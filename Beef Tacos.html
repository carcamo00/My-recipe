Classic Beef Tacos Recipe

These classic beef tacos are packed with flavor and perfect for family dinners. Seasoned ground beef baked in crispy taco shells and topped with melted cheese makes this dish simple, delicious, and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1½ teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1½ teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup water
  • 12 hard taco shells
  • 2 cups shredded Cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
  • Chopped tomatoes, lettuce, cilantro, red onion, sour cream, salsa, guacamole (for topping)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400℉.
  2. Cook the beef and onion in a large skillet over high heat until browned (5–7 minutes).
  3. Add cumin, oregano, chili powder, salt, pepper, tomato paste, garlic, and water.
  4. Reduce heat to medium and cook until mixture thickens (about 5 minutes).
  5. Divide taco meat between the 12 shells and place upright in a baking dish.
  6. Sprinkle cheese over the meat.
  7. Bake for about 10 minutes until cheese melts.
  8. Garnish with your favorite toppings and serve immediately.
